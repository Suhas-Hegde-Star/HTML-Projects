Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut

Back to Top 10 Fastest Cars

Overview

The Koenigsegg Jesko Absolut is the low-drag, top-speed variant of the Jesko, engineered for record-breaking velocity with a minimalist aerodynamic design. It boasts a drag coefficient of just 0.278, making it Koenigsegg's most aerodynamically efficient car ever built.

Engine and Performance

  • Engine: 5.0-liter twin-turbocharged V8 with a flat-plane crankshaft.
  • Power: 1,280 hp on regular gasoline and up to 1,600 hp on E85 renewable fuel.
  • Transmission: Revolutionary 9-speed Light Speed Transmission (LST) – flywheel-less for ultra-fast gear changes.
  • Ultimate Power On Demand (UPOD): Software that instantly selects the ideal gear for maximum acceleration.
  • World Records: 0–400–0 km/h time of 27.83 seconds, surpassing its own previous record.

Aerodynamics and Design

  • No active aerodynamics such as rear wings or splitters to reduce drag.
  • Smooth and streamlined body shape.
  • Rear hood fins designed to stabilize high-speed airflow.
  • Lightweight construction focused on achieving the highest possible top speed.
  • Rear-wheel drive delivering power efficiently at extreme velocities.
